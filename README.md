# Talking to Zotero

> *Automatically build a searchable knowledge base in Obsidian - straight from your Zotero library.*

---

## Dear Gentle Reader,

Ever remembered *something* - a finding, a sentence, a precise word - but have absolutely no idea which of the 338 papers in your library it came from? It is _somewhere_ in one of those files. Somewhere, but where?  

This is the solution to that.

These scripts (most lovingly generated by Claude Code) take everything in your Zotero library (annotations, highlights, notes, full PDFs) and turn them into structured and searchable Markdown files. They even create a Obsidian vault for them, but you can honestly just search through them anywhere (e.g. in your coding environment) with `Ctrl+F`.

You can download them to create your own amazing knowledge base - or take the existance of this as inspiration for your own projects, just as Maroš inspired mine.

Have fun,
Adrian

## How it works

Three scripts; zero magic (just nice Python).

| Script | What it does |
|---|---|
| `01_extract_zotero.py` | Reads your Zotero SQLite database - metadata, tags, annotations, notes |
| `02_extract_pdf_text.py` | Pulls full text from every PDF using PyMuPDF |
| `03_generate_notes.py` | Assembles everything into Obsidian-ready Markdown files |

Run them all at once:
```bash
./run_pipeline.sh            # normal (incremental) run
./run_pipeline.sh --force    # re-extract all PDFs from scratch
./run_pipeline.sh --dry-run  # preview without writing anything
```

## Getting started

1. **Read the code.** I feel obligated to note that you should really read through the code before running it. Who knows what amazing [Rick Rolls](https://streamable.com/lf027o) get downloaded otherwise on your computer?
2. **Adapt the config.** A handful of variables at the top of each file (e.g. your Zotero path, collection name, output folder). Alternatively: paste any script into the LLM of your choice and ask it to adapt it to your exact setup!
3. **Run it.** Feels like magic. I hope it inspires you to build even cooler applications!

## What you can do with this

- *"I remember this one small finding about X - give me the exact quote and which paper it's from."* Or even: *"Give me my note I made on that."*
- Full-text search across every paper you've ever annotated, in Obsidian or any editor
- Ask Claude (or any LLM) questions against your actual notes or facts from the pdf: *"Which input scaling did the varius ESNs in the different papers use? Please provide me with an overview, give me the precive quotes, and give me the BibTex keys."*
